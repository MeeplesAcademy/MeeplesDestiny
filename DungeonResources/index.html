Familiarity Breeds Confidence!

“Familiarity Breeds Confidence.” That’s my motto for Destiny’s dungeons. The more you know about a dungeon’s encounters, the more confidence you can have to control the encounter. The more you can control the room, the ‘easier’ the encounter can be.

What Does “Control the Room” Mean?

  • What are the mechanics that need to be done to unlock the room, or drop the boss’ immunity shield?
    • What steps do you need to take, in what order?
    • If the order doesn’t matter, what is optimal or feels like the best flow to you?
    • Meta doesn’t matter! What works for YOU?
  • Learn the enemy spawns!
    • What enemies start out in the encounter?
    • Which enemies spawn at its start and where do they spawn?
    • KEY: What triggers more enemy spawns during an encounter?
      • Mechanics: doing a thing spawns certain enemies.
      • Timer: certain enemies spawn every 30, 45, or 60 seconds, etc.
      • Kill: killing a certain enemy spawns a different enemy, OR killing all of a certain enemy respawns them all.
      • Knowing these triggers can help you have awareness of what’s about to happen so you can be ready. Control The Room!

For example: I died a million times solo in Pit of Heresy’s Chamber of Suffering encounter. It was overwhelming to me trying to kill the knights who drop the orbs that I needed to dunk, while making sure the central totem’s “anger timer” didn’t expire and kill me, all while trying to not be killed by the swarms of enemies who wouldn’t stop showing up. Then I learned the reasons. Certain enemies spawned on a timer in known locations. Certain enemies spawned after every dunk, in known locations. I could time killing a knight from a distance, get to the orb, pick it up, and check back on the totem to reset its wipe timer before dunking. All that, and a weapon which helped me clear the room quickly so I wasn’t always feeling the pressure from the hordes, made it doable. The encounter went from stressful chaos to manageable rotations, and my confidence soared. I was eventually able to get my first Solo Flawless of any dungeon in there. I went from panicking and thinking I’d never be able to do it solo, to being able to do it ‘easily’ regularly. All because I learned the encounter. “Familiarity Breeds Confidence.”

Some folks consider certain dungeons “easy.” I say “easy is relative.” Different players have different skill levels and experience with the game. One gamer might go into a dungeon solo for the very first time knowing nothing about the encounters and complete it solo flawless. Another might die a million times solo before they eventually complete it solo (or solo flawless). I cheer for them all.

If you’re on this page, I hope these resources help you the way they’ve helped me.
